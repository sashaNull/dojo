# Welcome to Pwnnsylvania! Pwnnsylvania is an educational platform for students and other cybersecurity enthusiasts to delve into and practice fundamental cybersecurity concepts through hands-on experiences. This platform is crafted to guide participants from cybersecurity novices to adept individuals capable of engaging in (simple) cybersecurity competitions (CTFs) and wargames. Operated by a [dedicated team](https://splab.cis.upenn.edu/) of skilled security researchers from the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn), Pwnnsylvania serves as a cornerstone for much of the university's cybersecurity curriculum. The platform is freely accessible to interested individuals worldwide. If you have any comments or feedback, we welcome you to reach out to us via email at [sebastian.angel@cis.upenn.edu](mailto:sebastian.angel@cis.upenn.edu)!

# Collaboration and Writeup Policy The challenges created for Pwnnsylvania are educational material, and are used to grade students at UPenn. Because of this, we would appreciate that writeups, walkthrough videos, and livestreams of challenge solutions are not posted to the internet.

# Further Practice After you learn the basics of cybersecurity through our courses, you should move on to harder challenges. Capture The Flags (CTFs) are a great way to practice your hacking skills in a fun and ethical way. The most popular way to find upcoming events is at [CTFtime](https://ctftime.org). There is also a list of introductory CTFs [here](https://zaratec.io/ctf-practice/). Wargames are another great way to practice your hacking skills. Whereas CTFs are short (normally 48 hour) events, wargames are not time-based. You can find a list of wargames at [the Wargame Nexus](https://github.com/zardus/wargame-nexus).
